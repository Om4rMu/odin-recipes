Masoob

Description

Hijazi Masoob (or Masoub) is a traditional dish from the Hijaz region of Saudi Arabia. It is a hearty and delicious dessert-like dish made from mashed bananas, torn-up bread (often flatbread or khubz), and a mix of cream, honey, and butter. Some versions also include toppings like nuts, raisins, or even a sprinkle of cinnamon for added flavor and texture.

Masoob is beloved for its sweet and comforting taste, making it a popular choice for breakfast or as a dessert. It’s also incredibly nourishing and filling, providing a great energy boost. The combination of natural sweetness from the bananas and the richness of butter and honey makes it a delightful treat that reflects the culinary heritage of the region.

Ingredients

  • 2 cup Brown Flour
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 2 tsps Oil
  • 1 3/4 cup Water
  • 6 pcs ripe bananas
  • 1 1/2 cup Powder Milk
  • 1 pcs Velor Canned Cream
  • 1/2 tbsp Ground Cardamom
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1 pcs Juice Lemon

Steps

  1. Step 1: In a bowl, sift flour, baking powder and salt. Add oil and water, knead to soft dough. Rest covered for 15 minutes.
  2. Step 2: Roll dough to a 23cm circle. Bake in a moderate oven on a greased pan. Cool. Cut bread into medium squares.
  3. Step 3: In a pan, mix cut bread, cardamom, milk, half amount honey and half amount banana. Cover and simmer for 3 minutes. Add half amount cream mix to corporate.
  4. step 4: Cut remaining banana. Pour lemon juice over banana to avoid color darkening. Add ¼ cup honey. Add banana mixture to pan, mix well.
  5. step 4: Put in serving plate. Make a hall in the centre, put remaining cream. Drizzle remaining honey. Serve.
