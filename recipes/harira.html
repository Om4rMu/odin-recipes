Harira

Description

Harira is a traditional Moroccan soup that is both hearty and flavorful, often enjoyed during Ramadan to break the fast. It’s made with a rich tomato base, tender lentils, chickpeas, and a mix of warming spices like cinnamon, ginger, and turmeric. The addition of fresh herbs, such as cilantro and parsley, gives it a vibrant taste, while thin noodles or rice add a satisfying texture.

Loved for its comforting qualities, Harira is versatile and can include lamb, chicken, or be made vegetarian. Typically served with dates, hard-boiled eggs, or bread, it embodies the warmth of Moroccan hospitality. This nourishing soup is not only a meal in itself but also a symbol of togetherness and tradition.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound cubed lamb meat
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 red onion, chopped
  • ¾ cup chopped celery
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons margarine
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 (29 ounce) can diced tomatoes, juice reserved
  • 7 cups water
  • ¾ cup green lentils
  • 1 (15 ounce) can garbanzo beans, drained
  • 4 ounces vermicelli pasta
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 lemon, juiced

Steps

  1. Step 1: Combine lamb, onions, celery, cilantro, margarine, pepper, turmeric, cinnamon, ginger, and cayenne in a large soup pot over low heat. Cook, stirring frequently, for 5 minutes. Pour in tomatoes (without juice) and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Step 2: Add water, reserved tomato juice, and lentils. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 1 hour 50 minutes.
  3. Step 3: Increase the heat to medium-high and add garbanzo beans and vermicelli pasta. Cook until noodles are tender yet firm to the bite, about 10 minutes. Stir in eggs and lemon juice and cook for 1 minute.
